<title id="_a4a1287c-43ad-be97-f71c-e973d3ec0a50">Practical realization of radiometric units</title>
<p id="_7ff82f94-11e5-d1d0-643f-ac851e389d67">There are two types of primary methods in general use for realizing radiometric units. These are referred to as “<tt>detector-based</tt>” or “<tt>source-based</tt>” depending on whether they rely on a primary standard detector or primary standard source, respectively.</p>

<title id="_6b8bcd9a-da65-e270-8c64-e3e153adb795">Traceability routes for the practical realization of photometric units</title>
<p id="_07cfa71e-8d0a-5ef3-739d-50e07f6543cd"> <xref target="sec-5"/> outlines the traceability routes for practical realization of radiometric units. As described in <xref target="sec-2"/>, these underpin the realization of photometric units associated with the corresponding photometric quantities (e.g. luminous intensity, luminance, illuminance, luminous flux) through the use of spectral luminous efficiency functions (to provide a spectral weighting) combined with the luminous efficacy at a frequency of <asciimath>540 xx 10^12 "‌" "unitsml(Hz)"</asciimath>, as given in the definition of <asciimath>ii(K)_{"cd"}</asciimath>. The lowest uncertainty for the realization of photometric units is currently achieved if the traceability chain starts with an absolute detector, but in the future could be achieved by using an absolute source or the photon counting approach. For more details see <eref type="inline" bibitemid="zwinkels" citeas="[5]"/> and references therein.</p>
